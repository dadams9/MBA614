Big Boggle

Big Boggle is a word-discovery game originally produced by Parker Brothers (Google "big boggle rules" for details of game play). In play, the user is presented with a randomly-ordered 5 by 5 grid of cubes--each with one face showing. The goal is to find as many words (subject to the game rules) as possible in a three minute round. Full instructions are online at: http://www.hasbro.com/common/instruct/BigBoggle.PDF You can see a video of how Boggle is played at http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ntZVkJEArEg. This video is for the regular Boggle (16 letter cubes) however, you assignment is for Big Boggle (25 letter cubes).

2 70 So, each of the 25 cubes has 6 letters, the first has the letters A, F, I, R, S, and Y on the cube. On any given round, exactly one of the faces of each cube will be showing. There is only one Z and one X and they happen to be on the same cube, so no round can have both a Z and an X in play simultaneously, which means that he word "zax" can never be formed.  By the way, a zax is a knife for splitting roof slate.  It's a great word to know for scrabble and othe word games; it just won't help you in Big Boggle.

In the provided workbook, write a procedure that completes the assignment by making use of the 5 by 5 grid on the sheet named "Boggle".   Be sure there is exactly one letter from each "cube" showing in each cell of the grid. The positioning of each cube within the grid should also be random.

You must make use of at least one array as a fundamental part of your solution.  Your solution must be built entirely in VBA. That is, you may not write data to a worksheet to build your solution nor may your solution read data from any worksheet.  

Hint:  Notice that there is a range named "game" on the "Boggle" sheet.  Remember that you can refer to a named range with the following syntax:

Range("game")

Also remember that you can refer to cells in a range with a single index with the "cells" method.  So, even though the "game" range is a 5x5 grid, you can easily put a value in the 17th cell (row 4, column 2) with the following syntax:

Range("game").cells(17).value="A"

Note:  Technically, one cube face has two letters on it: Qu.  For this assignment you may ignore this inconsistency and just put a "Q" on that face.  However, if you are up to the challenge, see if you can make a "u" always go with the "Q."  Accomplishing this will not earn you any points but it will give you great satisfaction.
